About Turtles

Turtles are ancient reptiles that have roamed the Earth for over 200 million years, surviving mass extinctions and adapting to various environments. From the depths of the ocean to dry deserts, these fascinating creatures play a vital role in maintaining the balance of their ecosystems.

The Role of Turtles in Ecosystems

Sea turtles help maintain healthy seagrass beds and coral reefs, while freshwater turtles control insect populations. Tortoises aid in seed dispersal, helping to shape the landscapes they inhabit.

Why Conservation Matters

Turtles face numerous threats, including habitat destruction, pollution, and illegal poaching. By learning about these issues, we can all play a part in protecting these incredible creatures for future generations.
